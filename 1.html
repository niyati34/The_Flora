Frequently Asked Questions

To choose the right plants for your garden, consider your local climate, sunlight exposure, soil type, and your personal preferences. Research the specific requirements of each plant before making a selection.

The frequency of watering depends on the type of plant, the season, and the climate. Most plants prefer to be watered when the top inch of soil is dry. It's essential to avoid overwatering, as it can lead to root rot.

If your plant has pests, isolate it from other plants to prevent the infestation from spreading. Identify the type of pest and treat it accordingly. Options include insecticidal soap, neem oil, or manual removal.

The best time to fertilize plants is during the growing season, typically in the spring and summer. Use a balanced, slow-release fertilizer and follow the recommended dosage on the product label.

To protect your plants from extreme weather, consider using mulch, shade cloth, or row covers. Water your plants adequately during hot periods and provide frost protection during cold snaps.

Yellowing leaves can be a sign of various issues, including overwatering, underwatering, nutrient deficiency, or pests. Investigate the specific cause and take appropriate measures, such as adjusting watering or providing the necessary nutrients.
